About Us

It is no secret that science governs the very essence of life. From the streets we walk on to the nature that surrounds us, many intricate principles are ever-present in the world we live in. As such, Innovative Minds is dedicated to bringing all things science and environment to the students of Asia Pacific University. What began as a club of 4 members has now grown into the prestigious community of like-minded science enthusiast whose effort to provide quality content is well-knwon among the students and faculty alike. Throughout the years, we have established many connections with other parties on and off campus. The fruits of our labour have allowed us to gradually organise more events for the public in hopes of cultivating intellectual minds, further progressing the the nation's growth in challenging environments. In addition to science, we are also environmental activists and therefore conduct charity events to safeguard Mother Nature as well.


Steady Growth with Much Support

In 2020, our science and environment club has a total of 250 members and over 50 volunteering tutors. The feedback provided by those affiliated with our club has made room for tremendous improvements. We take pride in the fact that our humble club is even recognised by learning institutes across the country. This is indeed a testament to the force of sheer determination and excellent teamwork combined!

Milestones

Going The Distance, One Step at a Time.



Sea Turtle Conservation

In 2017, our club has collaborated with the Turtle Conservation Society of Malaysia to protect the endangered leatherback sea turtle along the coast of Lang Tengah. Our members patrolled the coast at night to collect the sea turtle eggs, bringing the haul to the nearby sea turtle conservation center. To acknowledge our initiative, the officials of the society has awarded us with a certificate.



Champion of International Science Competition 2017

In 2019, the veterans of our club who has in-depth knowledge on science had entered the Regeneron International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF) to compete with the elites from around the globe. After much effort, the team was awarded second place with a prize money of $50,000, and their success was even featured in the local papers. Much to our delight, their achievement has brought much needed recognition to our club in this age where technology as we know it is ever-evolving
